| **High** | `execute()` generator – entire body | No top‑level try‑catch. Any uncaught exception (e.g., in `fastPath.process`, `flash.select`, `toolImpl.execute` if not caught) terminates the generator with a thrown error, which may become an unhandled rejection and leaves the SSE stream / client hanging without an error event. | Wrap the whole generator logic in a try‑catch that yields an `error` event (with a code) and then returns. |
| **High** | `execute()` – tool execution & `startMemoryLookup()` – network/embedding calls | No timeout on external operations (`embed()`, `vectorStore.search()`, `tool.execute()`). | **High** | Constructor – `vectorStore.load()`, used in `startMemoryLookup()` | `vectorStore.load()` is called without `await`; the generator can start (`execute()` → `startMemoryLookup()`) before the store finishes loading. | **Medium** | `execute()` – all async branches | No cancellation support. If the client closes the SSE connection (calling `.return()` on the async iterator), pending tool executions or memory lookups continue running in the background, wasting resources and possibly causing side‑effects. | Accept an `AbortSignal` in `execute()`; pass it to internal operations, abort on generator return, and stop ongoing work. |
